# Cole Ruud-Johnson: Sourcing Land Without MLS — Deep Dive

> **YouTube / REtipster / Industry** · Research Brief · `land` · 2026-04-06

## TL;DR

Follow-up deep dive on Cole's land sourcing methods. Key alpha: SMS + handwritten mail outperforms mass marketing, The Land Portal beats national data providers, paper rehabilitation (fixing title issues) produces highest margins, and survey hacking with minor subdivisions lets you pay sellers 80-90% of market value while still profiting. Market not saturated — high churn keeps it open for serious operators.

## Key Insights

- **SMS + handwritten mail > mass marketing.** Handwritten mailers produce less hostile, more negotiable leads with higher signed-deal-to-contract ratios. 2-3x easier to generate land leads vs. house leads via cold calling.
- **The Land Portal is the best data source.** Same underlying data as major platforms but more accessible for direct-to-seller targeting. Integration opportunity with n8n/OpenClaw for automated property alerts.
- **Paper rehabilitation > physical improvements.** Highest margins from fixing legal/title issues — affidavit of heirship, back taxes, liens, quiet title. "Rich Uncle" positioning: solving complex title issues and writing large checks.
- **Subdivision unlocks premium pricing.** Buy large tracts, split into smaller parcels. Allows paying 80-90% of market (vs. competitors at 50%) because post-subdivision value is significantly higher.
- **Market not actually saturated.** High churn (most quit 30-60 days), no institutional lending, no big banks, limited software ecosystem. That gap = opportunity for serious operators who treat it as a 50-year business.

**The real edge in land isn't finding deals — it's paper rehabilitation and subdivision. Fixing title issues and splitting large tracts creates value that simple flipping never can, and the market's high churn rate ensures consistent deal flow for operators who stick.**
