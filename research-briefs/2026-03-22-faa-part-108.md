# FAA Part 108 — BVLOS Rule That Reshapes the UAS Industry

> **FAA / Federal Register** · Regulatory Brief · `uas` · 2026-03-22

---

## TL;DR

Part 108 is the FAA's 700-page proposed rule (NPRM Aug 2025) to normalize routine BVLOS drone operations — replacing today's waiver-only regime with scalable permits and certificates. Final rule expected March-April 2026 per Executive Order, 6-12 month implementation after. Two flashpoints dominate: Section 108.700's country-of-origin restriction (de facto DJI ban) and modified right-of-way rules that alarm manned aviation. Part 107 stays for VLOS; Part 108 adds a new organizational-accountability layer on top.

## Key Insights

- **Two-tier authorization.** Operating permits for lower-risk BVLOS; operating certificates for complex ops (delivery, dense urban, larger UAS). New mandatory roles: Operations Supervisor (org-level) and Flight Coordinator (per-flight). Part 107 cert remains the prerequisite — pilots add a 108 endorsement.
- **Section 108.700 is the grenade.** Country-of-origin requirement limits eligibility to US-manufactured drones or those from countries with bilateral UAS agreements — which currently number zero. Effectively bars DJI and all foreign platforms. Public safety agencies, first responders, and 80%+ of commercial operators are loudly opposed.
- **Right-of-way modifications scare manned aviation.** AOPA, NBAA, ALPA, and HAI warn the proposed rules dilute protections for helicopters, air ambulances, and business aircraft in low-altitude airspace. Vertical Aviation International says the rule "will create, not mitigate, midair collision potential."
- **No grandfathering path.** Operators holding existing Part 107 BVLOS waivers have no clear transition mechanism to Part 108. Industry associations are pushing hard for a streamlined conversion pathway.
- **Test ranges become proving grounds.** The performance-based framework means operators must demonstrate compliance — test ranges are where that evidence gets generated. AirSpace Integration's facility is exactly the infrastructure this rule needs to function.
