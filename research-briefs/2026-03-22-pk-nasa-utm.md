# PK (Parimal Kopardekar) — NASA UTM Architect

> **NASA Ames** · Person Brief · `uas` · 2026-03-22

---

## TL;DR

PK invented UAS Traffic Management (UTM) at NASA Ames in 2013 — the globally adopted architecture for managing drone traffic below 400ft. He's now AAM Mission Integration Manager + NARI Director, bridging UTM into BVLOS corridors and Advanced Air Mobility. Won the Sammies, NASA Software of the Year, and Government Invention of the Year for UTM. At LIFT he'll likely speak on the Day 2 "Digital Airspace Infrastructure" panel. He co-led NASA's wildfire UAS study — ask about that.

## Key Insights

- **UTM is his baby.** He conceived it in 2013, published the foundational NASA technical memo in 2014, ran four Technology Capability Level (TCL) test campaigns through 2019, then handed the operational framework to FAA. The architecture uses UAS Service Suppliers (USS) as commercial middlemen under FAA authority — same model the BVLOS rule (Part 108) will codify.
- **Triple crown.** 2018 Sammies "Promising Innovations" medal, 2019 NASA Software of the Year (UTM software), 2020 NASA Government Invention of the Year (UTM patent). Global patent holder.
- **Now running AAM integration.** As Mission Integration Manager he coordinates all ARMD activities toward a validated AAM system architecture — eVTOL corridors, autonomy certification, vertiport airspace. UTM BVLOS subproject wrapped Feb 2026; results feeding directly into FAA Part 108.
- **Wildfire is his passion project.** Co-led NASA's wildfire mitigation needs assessment. His pitch: smoke grounds manned aircraft after 4-8 hours/day — autonomous UAS could extend aerial firefighting to 24/7. California audience will eat this up.
- **Test ranges are his proving ground.** UTM was validated at FAA-designated test sites (Nevada, Virginia, North Dakota, NY 50-mile corridor). AirSpace Integration's test range is the exact type of facility his architecture was designed to serve.
