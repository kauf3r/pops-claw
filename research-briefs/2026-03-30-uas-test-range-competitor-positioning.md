# UAS Test Range Value Proposition — Competitor Positioning Analysis

> **FAA / Industry Research** · Strategic Brief · `uas` · 2026-03-30

---

## TL;DR

All 9 FAA-designated test sites offer fundamentally similar services. Differentiation is won on specialization, proof points, and ecosystem access. AirSpace Integration is NOT an FAA test site — it's a commercial test range with different customers and value props. Three positioning opportunities: California coastal advantage, ASTM F3322 TPTA testing specialization, and Part 108 permit support.

## Key Insights

- **FAA has 9 designated test sites (2026).** All offer BVLOS COA access, safety case support, and FAA policy contribution. Two added in 2026 (Oklahoma, Indiana). Market is mature and crowded at the federal level.
- **Three competitive moats observed.** (1) Regulatory innovation track record (Virginia Tech MAAP), (2) Unique physical infrastructure (North Dakota Vantis 3K-mile network, NY SkyDome), (3) Ecosystem access (Alaska Arctic ops, Nevada urban AAM).
- **AirSpace plays a different game.** Commercial test range ≠ FAA designated site. Different customer, different value prop. Don't create confusion by implying FAA designation.
- **California coastal ops is a unique niche.** Only West Coast range with immediate ocean access. California regulatory complexity (CPUC, Coastal Commission) is a barrier competitors don't address.
- **ASTM F3322 TPTA testing is unclaimed territory.** None of the 9 FAA sites emphasize Third-Party Test Authority services as a core differentiator. Defensible niche.
- **None of the 9 FAA test sites exhibited at LIFT Summit.** Market opportunity for AirSpace as "the local test range" for Monterey Bay operators.

## The One Thing

**AirSpace Integration wins by owning a niche the FAA sites ignore — ASTM F3322 TPTA testing + California coastal operations — not by trying to out-general-purpose the designated test sites.**
