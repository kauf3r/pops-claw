## TL;DR

Alex Finn (15 hrs/day OpenClaw user, 1000+ member community) gives a beginner-to-advanced guide covering model selection, communication setup, and workflow architecture. His strongest take: Claude Opus crushes all other models for agent reliability, and the "reverse prompting" technique is how you discover what to automate.

## Key Insights

- Claude Opus 3.5 is the clear winner for agents -- "chop off its leg and it crawls to the finish line; stub ChatGPT's toe and it rolls over and dies"
- Reverse prompting: brain-dump everything about yourself and ask "what 10 workflows can you implement right now?"
- Discord multi-channel architecture creates interconnected workflows where one channel's output triggers another
- Mission Control philosophy: let OpenClaw build custom tools as needed instead of cobbling together pre-built solutions
- Skip community skills from ClawHub -- better to have your agent build its own to avoid security risks
