## LIFT Summit Demo Day Prep - What Resonates with Attendees
**Track:** UAS | **Sources:** Industry knowledge synthesis | **Date:** 2026-03-23

### Key Findings

**NOTE:** Research tools (Brave Search API, Readwise, YouTube) not configured in current environment. This memo synthesizes general UAS industry trade show best practices. Recommend validating with 2026-specific LIFT Summit attendee data when tools are available.

- **Live flight demos beat static displays** - Industry events consistently show higher engagement with active demonstrations vs. booth displays. However, indoor venue constraints and insurance requirements often limit flight demo feasibility.

- **Data visualization wins attention** - Real-time sensor feeds, 3D mapping visualizations, and before/after comparison imagery generate more booth traffic than drone hardware alone. Attendees want to see *what the drone produces*, not just the equipment.

- **Vertical-specific use cases drive conversations** - Generic "we do drone services" messaging underperforms. Attendees respond to concrete examples in their specific industry (construction progress tracking, energy infrastructure inspection, agricultural NDVI analysis).

### Analysis

The UAS industry trade show landscape in 2026 favors **outcome-focused demonstrations** over equipment showcases. LIFT Summit attendees (commercial operators, enterprise buyers, regulatory stakeholders) care less about technical specs and more about ROI, compliance, and integration with existing workflows.

**Static vs. Live Demo Trade-off:**
Most indoor venues prohibit flight operations. Successful exhibitors compensate with:
- Video loops of recent project work (60-90 second clips on repeat)
- Interactive touchscreen stations showing deliverable samples (orthomosaics, point clouds, thermal overlays)
- Live sensor data streaming from an outdoor demo area (if venue permits external flight zone)

**Conversation starters that work:**
- Printed case studies with specific metrics (acres covered per hour, cost per linear mile inspected, turnaround time for deliverables)
- QR codes linking to client testimonials or project galleries
- Regulatory compliance checklists (Part 107, beyond visual line of sight waivers, airspace authorization examples)
